## 0. Project Description (Optional but Recommended)

```
[BRIEF OVERVIEW IN 2-3 SENTENCES]

Example: "This project encompasses the planning and execution of our wedding celebration. 
We envision a [STYLE] event that brings together [NUMBER] of our closest family and friends 
to celebrate our commitment. The planning will span [TIMEFRAME] with a focus on [KEY PRIORITIES]."
```

**Why include this:**
- Sets context for the entire charter
- Helps clarify vision early
- Useful for stakeholders (families, vendors, coordinators)
- Prevents miscommunication about overall intent

**Examples by wedding type:**

- **Intimate outdoor**: "This project encompasses planning an intimate outdoor garden wedding for 40 guests. We envision a relaxed, personal celebration with close family and friends, emphasizing authenticity and natural beauty. Planning spans 6 months with a focus on simplicity and meaningful moments."

- **Formal black-tie**: "This project encompasses executing a formal black-tie wedding reception for 150 guests at an upscale venue. We envision an elegant, sophisticated celebration that honors both families. Planning spans 9 months with a focus on flawless execution and memorable experiences."

- **Destination wedding**: "This project encompasses planning a destination wedding for 60 guests in [LOCATION]. We envision a unique experience that combines wedding celebration with a group gathering. Planning spans 8 months with a focus on coordinating travel, accommodations, and an unforgettable celebration."

- **Small backyard**: "This project encompasses hosting a casual backyard wedding for 25 close friends and family. We envision a relaxed, DIY celebration that reflects our personalities. Planning spans 3 months with a focus on budget-consciousness and personal touches."

## 2. Project Goal
```
Plan and execute a [STYLE] wedding celebration for [NUMBER] guests on [DATE] at [VENUE], 
ensuring [KEY OUTCOME] while maintaining [PRIORITY] throughout the planning and execution process.
```

**Placeholder guidance:**
- STYLE: (e.g., "intimate outdoor", "formal black-tie", "casual backyard", "destination")
- NUMBER: (guest count)
- DATE: (specific date)
- VENUE: (location or type)
- KEY OUTCOME: (e.g., "meaningful celebration of commitment", "memorable experience for loved ones", "stress-free day")
- PRIORITY: (e.g., "authenticity", "budget discipline", "family involvement", "simplicity")

**Example**: Plan and execute an intimate outdoor wedding celebration for 75 guests on June 15, 2026 at Riverside Estate, 
ensuring a meaningful celebration of our commitment while maintaining budget discipline ($[X]) throughout planning and execution.

## 7. High-Level Requirements

```
PLANNING REQUIREMENTS:
- Define wedding style/theme and communicate to all vendors
- Establish budget and track spending in real-time
- Select and contract [X] key vendors (list: catering, photography, etc.)
- Manage guest list with RSVP tracking
- Create timeline and day-of schedule for all stakeholders
- Plan ceremony (vows, music, readings, order of events)
- Design reception experience (seating, menu, entertainment, timeline)

LOGISTICS REQUIREMENTS:
- Secure venue agreement and confirm availability
- Arrange guest parking and accommodations if multi-location
- Create contingency plans (weather, vendor no-show, schedule delays)
- Brief wedding party on roles and schedule
- Coordinate with vendors on setup/breakdown

EXECUTION REQUIREMENTS:
- Designate day-of coordinator or point person
- Brief ceremony participants day-before or day-of
- Manage timeline flexibility (delays, on-the-fly decisions)
- Capture photography/videography per shot list
- Ensure vendor arrival and setup per schedule
```

## 8. Risks & Assumptions

```
RISKS:
- Budget overruns: Vendors charge more than quoted, unexpected costs arise
  Mitigation: Lock in prices in contracts, build [X]% contingency buffer
  
- Vendor cancellation: Key vendor unavailable on wedding date
  Mitigation: Backup vendors identified, penalty clauses in contracts, insurance considered
  
- Guest logistics: Late RSVPs, last-minute cancellations, parking/accommodation issues
  Mitigation: RSVP deadline enforced, waitlist managed, clear logistics guide sent
  
- Weather disruption: Rain/extreme weather impacts outdoor plans
  Mitigation: Tent rental, indoor backup location, contingency schedule
  
- Schedule slippage: Ceremony/reception runs behind, timeline compresses
  Mitigation: Buffer time built in, clear vendor communication, day-of coordinator empowered to decide
  
- Scope creep: Additional guests, additional events, expanded vision mid-planning
  Mitigation: Guest list frozen at [DATE], decisions documented, change request process defined

ASSUMPTIONS:
```

## Tips for Filling In

- **Be specific**: "June 15, 2026 at Riverside Estate" beats "summer wedding"
- **Define success early**: "75 guests" and "within $X budget" are measurable
- **Name people**: "Mom will handle floral design" vs "someone will handle flowers"
- **Lock scope**: Clear "in/out" prevents scope creep mid-planning
- **Budget buffer**: Add 10-15% contingency for unexpected costs
- **Contingency plans**: For outdoor weddings, always have indoor backup
- **Timeline dates**: Work backward from wedding date (6 months = venue + saves, 3 months = invites, etc.)

---

## Customization Ideas

**For destination weddings:**
- Add travel coordination, visa requirements, multi-time-zone logistics

**For small intimate weddings (< 30 people):**
- Simplify vendor list, less formal timeline, more flexible schedule

**For large formal weddings (> 150 people):**
- Add more vendor roles (day-of coordinator, planner, valet, lighting)
- Build longer timeline (8-12 months recommended)
- Add seating chart complexity and logistics

**For budget-conscious weddings:**
- Emphasize DIY elements, vendor negotiation, phased spending
- Add cost-tracking requirement to success criteria
