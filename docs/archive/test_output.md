# Executive Summary

## Critical Care Volume Growth Drives Staffing Need

The CVICU has experienced substantial growth in high-acuity mechanical circulatory support devices over the past 21 months, creating an urgent need for additional specialized nursing staff. Two key device categories demonstrate this accelerating demand.

### MCS Device Growth

**MCS device utilization increased 67%** from the January 2024 baseline to October 2025:

| Period | Devices in Use | Growth from Baseline |
|--------|---------------|---------------------|
| Jan 2024 | 9 | Baseline (0%) |
| Feb 2024 | 10 | +11% |
| Mar 2024 | 11 | +22% |
| Apr 2024 | 12 | +33% |
| May 2024 | 13 | +44% |
| Jun 2024 | 14 | +56% |
| Oct 2025 | 15 | **+67%** |

**Calculation:** Growth = (15 - 9) / 9 = **67% increase**

This represents a consistent upward trajectory, with the unit adding **6 additional MCS devices** over 21 months—an average of approximately **0.3 devices per month**. Each MCS device requires dedicated 1:1 nursing coverage and specialized competency.

### ECMO Utilization Surge

**ECMO device usage doubled** over the same timeframe, showing even more dramatic growth:

| Period | Monthly Device Range | Midpoint | Growth from Baseline |
|--------|---------------------|----------|---------------------|
| Jan 2024 | 2-3 devices | 2.5 | Baseline (0%) |
| Mar 2024 | 3-4 devices | 3.5 | +40% |
| Jun 2024 | 4-5 devices | 4.5 | +80% |
| Oct 2025 | 5-6 devices | 5.5 | **+120%** |

**Calculation:** Growth = (5.5 - 2.5) / 2.5 = **120% increase**

ECMO represents the highest acuity level in the CVICU, requiring **continuous bedside presence** from specially trained nurses. The unit has added **3 additional ECMO patients monthly** on average, more than doubling baseline capacity.

### Code Blue Events: Stable Baseline

Code Blue events have remained **consistent at 45-75 events per month** throughout 2024-2025 with no significant upward or downward trend. This stability indicates that general emergency response volume is **not a driver** for this staffing request—the need stems specifically from sustained growth in planned, high-acuity device therapies.

### Staffing Implications

The combined **67% growth in MCS** and **120% growth in ECMO** devices creates a compounding staffing challenge:

• Both device types require **1:1 nurse-to-patient ratios** due to complexity and patient instability  
• Specialized training and competency validation are mandatory for staff managing these devices  
• Growth has been **sustained and consistent**, not a temporary spike, indicating permanent capacity expansion  
• Current staffing levels were calibrated to January 2024 baseline volumes, now exceeded by **6-9 additional high-acuity patients** at any given time

This data demonstrates that the CVICU has fundamentally expanded its service
