#

1. Project Goal

**Desired Outcome:**
The primary goal of the 'HFW Renegotiation' project is to establish a new contract with HFW that aligns with TPCCC's current practice and operational dynamics. The renegotiated contract aims to address the current situation where many unsubsidized providers operate in the hospital, providing substantial benefits to HFW at significant cost to TPCCC. These benefits include Interventional Pulmonary/Lung Nodule Support and other initiatives rendered free of charge.

**Success Measures:**
The success of the 'HFW Renegotiation' project will be determined by the successful renegotiation and implementation of a new contract. This new contract should address the issues identified in the existing contract, particularly those related to staffing and subsidy provisions. The finalized contract should meet TPCCC's operational requirements and ensure a sustainable and mutually beneficial agreement with HFW.

The problem at hand is the current contract with the hospital, which requires TPCCC to manage the PCU patients. In addition, there are requests from other services, particularly Cardiothoracic Surgery, for an expanded intensivist presence in the cardiac ICU. To accommodate these changes, an increase in the current contract value is needed, which necessitates a comprehensive contract renegotiation.
